Note:

Fair Value(FV): For the sectors which have at least 10 companies, fair value is the weighted average relative value per share of the company calculated by using respective sector PE (50%), PB (20%) and PS (30%) ratios. For the sectors consisting of less than ten companies, fair value is the weighted average relative value per share of the company calculated by using market PE (50%), PB (20%) and PS (30%) ratios. If P/E ratio is missing for a company, only P/B is used to calculate the Fair Value of that company. If P/E and P/B ratio both are missing for a company, N/A is written in the cell of Fair Value of that company.
Trailing P/E ratio: The trailing P/E is calculated by dividing the current share price by the total EPS over the past 12 months.
P/B ratio: It is calculated by dividing the current share price by the book value per share (BVPS). Basically, it measures the market's valuation of a stock relative to its book value.
P/S ratio: It is calculated by dividing the stock price by the underlying company's total sales per share over the past 12 months. It shows how much investors are willing to pay per dollar of sales for a stock.
D/E ratio: It is calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its shareholder equity for manufacturing companies. On the other hand, for Banks & NBFIs it is calculated by dividing total tier 1 capital by total exposure/total risk-weighted assets.For insurance companies, the leverage ratio is assumed Net premiums/ shareholders’ equity.
EPS: It is calculated as a company's net profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock.
Dividend Yield: It is a financial ratio expressed as a percentage that shows how much a company pays out in dividends each year relative to its stock price.
Up/Down Ratio:The upside/downside ratio is the volume traded in advancing vs. declining issues on a given trading day.
Return: A holding period return is an investment's return over the time it is owned by a particular investor. It is the percentage change between the current price of a stock and the beginning price of the stock from the holding period.
Daily Volume: It means how many shares are traded per day.
EPS CAGR: It means the cumulative average growth rate of the earnings per share (EPS) over a period.
LTP: It means the last traded price of the stock.
